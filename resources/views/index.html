Dare to be Different

Anokha, the tech fest of Amrita School of Engineering, Coimbatore, is the 3-day congregation of some of the brightest minds in India. Founded in 2010, Anokha has grown by leaps and bounds and has progressed to become one of the largest tech fests in South India. It witnesses a variety of events and workshops in different domains like Engineering, Business, Social Media, Finance, etc. Attractions like the talk series- Lumière, Anokha's MUN edition- LeadersMeet, Anokha Expo and the pro-show night add to its charm.

The fest aims to bring the best out of students by providing them an invaluable opportunity to discover, develop and demonstrate their talents (and have a great time doing it). This year, we are determined to do the same... only bigger and better!

to go.

By Air
The closest airport is in Coimbatore, located about 27 kilometers from the campus. Most airlines operate flights to Coimbatore from all major cities in India.

By Bus
The nearest major bus terminals are at Gandhipuram (about 22 kms) and Ukkadam (about 18 kms). There are regular bus services from Gandhipuram (Bus Nos. 96, A3 and 3G) and Ukkadam (Bus Nos. 96, 48, S2, 3G and A3). Alight at Ettimadai Piruvu Bus Stop (Amrita College) and hire an auto rickshaw to the campus (3 kms). A3 bus will come upto the University Gate. The travel time from Ukkadam to Ettimadai Piruvu is around 45 minutes.

Ettimadai is a request stop for all Kerala and Tamil Nadu State Transport buses from Kerala. Alight at Ettimadai Piruvu Bus Stop (Amrita College) and hire an auto rickshaw (costs Rs.40) to the campus (3 kms). Travel time from Palakkad to Ettimadai Piruvu is around one hour. In addition to this, shuttle bus service will be available to and from the campus on the days of the fest.

By Train
Passenger trains are available from Coimbatore Railway Station and Palghat Railway Station. Ettimadai Railway Station is only about 500 metres from the campus and one may easily walk to campus upon alighting at the station.

      Arun Krishnamurthy

      Environmental activist, Founder at Environmentalist Foundation of India


      Arun Krishnamurthy completed his Bachelor's in microbiology from Madras Christian College and worked at Google, Hyderabad for over three years. Arun then went to study in The Indian Institute of Mass Communication in Delhi. Arun founded the NGO, Environmentalist Foundation of India (EFI) in 2011 based in Chennai which has its branches in other cities such as Hyderabad, Delhi and Coimbatore. Arun with the organisation has been successful in cleaning over 17 lakes in the country. He was chosen as among the five people for prestigious Rolex Awards for Enterprise award in 2012 for scientific lake restoration in Chennai. During the 2015 floods in Chennai and east coast of TamilNadu, Environmentalist Foundation of India was actively involved in flood relief work across suburban Chennai and rural pockets of Cuddalore District. Through their 3 stage approach the organization worked on rescue, rehabilitation and rebuilding of flood affected regions.

      "Lake Kilkattalai, which feeds the Pallikaranai wetland, is home to several species of birds and pond turtles. It is being choked. If it continues this way, 20 years down the line, this lake could become a forgotten story. I am determined not to let that happen."


      Armstrong Pame

      Officer of the Indian Administrative Service


      Armstrong Pame is the sub-divisional magistrate of Tamenglong, his home district in Manipur, and the first IAS officer from the Zeme-naga tribe. He propelled to fame in media for beginning an initiative to build a 100-km motorable road involving the local community, without any government funding. Named the "Tamenglong-Haflong Road" it links Manipur with Nagaland and Assam. He started a Facebook group to raise money for the project and slowly donations poured in for the project from all around the world. In August 2012, he raised INR 40 Lakh for the purpose through a Facebook page. For his efforts in building the road with donations and volunteers he was invited to Facebook's headquarters in California. In 2012, he was nominated for CNN-IBN Indian of the Year in Public service category. Dubbed the "miracle man" by the villagers, he garners vibes of respect for the letters I.A.S.

      "The journey of life has roads which connect the heart."


      Bhakti Sharma

      Indian swimmer, world record holder


      Bhakti Sharma is an open water swimmer. Born in Mumbai and brought up in Udaipur, her mother started teaching her to swim from when she was two and a half years old. She is one of the few people in the world to have swum across the English Channel. Along with her Mother-Coach Leena Sharma and friend Priyanka Gehlot, Bhakti holds the Asian record for the first swim by a 3-member women's relay team across the Channel. She is only the third person in the world to have swum in the Arctic Ocean, and recently swam in the Antarctic Ocean to become the youngest to swim in all five oceans, a feat which earned her recognition from Prime Minister Narendra Modi. Bhakti's story tells us about the fruit that perseverance yields and how human endurance exists to be tested.

      "I was afraid but the good thing about learning at such an early age was that 4ft and 6ft deep is all the same to a kid. I have been swimming for as long as I can remember."


      Harish Sivaramakrishnan

      UI Engineer, Rockstar


      Harish Sivaramakrishnan is a product expert, a designer, a rock-band member and most relatably a UI engineer. He has striked a balance in his life that most of consider only mythical and unrealizable. To begin elaborating about Harish, he is currently the Vice president of Design at Snapdeal. He is also the front man for popular Carnatic Progressive rock band, Agam. He's had a long career being a UI expert and a software developer, working with Adobe, Myntra, Freecharge and now Snapdeal while also being behind the scenes in the creation of a new music genre. He is the rare blend of technology and art that we seldom hear about in a country where chasing one passion means sacrificing another.

      "Time is in your mind, man. When you have a harmony and equilibrium about what you do, every minute is worth more. For example, I don't think of the gig I have to play, when I'm working on a UX problem. You just need to compartmentalize these things."


      Mani Vajipeyajula

      CEO & Founder at Banyan Nation


      Banyan is a high technology social venture that takes an integrated approach to recycling by innovating in solid waste management techniques. The organization is headed by Mr. Mani who saw the opportunity improve the quality of recycled materials, and support worker safety within the country's disjointed and largely informal recycling sector. He has dual M.B.A.s from Columbia Business School and UC Berkley. He knew from his early days at Berkeley that he wanted to do something good for India and decided, based on prior visits here, to tackle his home country's waste-management woes. Banyan is currently focusing on plastics and already has a plant processing 1.5 to 2 tons of it daily.

      "We aim to create legal recycling processes that people are able to understand and see."
